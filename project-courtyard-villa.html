Courtyard Villa

Ahmedabad — a family home organised around a shaded pool court, with deep overhangs and cross-ventilated living spaces tuned for the western sun.

Brief & context

Three generations under one roof, with clear separation between public entertaining zones and quiet family bedrooms. The plot allowed a north–south elongation; the challenge was heat gain from the west façade and managing monsoon splash across the pool edge.

The client asked for gallery-like interiors without feeling cold — stone underfoot, lime plaster walls, and teak joinery with bronze hardware.

Design response

  • 01

    Court-first planning

    Living, dining, and kitchen wrap the pool on the east and north; bedrooms sit in a quieter wing with smaller openings to the west.

  • 02

    Monsoon detailing

    Overflow channels, drip edges, and ventilated stone cladding at plinth level keep finishes dry through heavy rain.

  • 03

    Material calm

    Limited palette — kota stone, teak, lime plaster, and bronze — repeated at thresholds so spaces read as one continuous composition.

  • 04

    Light & privacy

    Adjustable timber screens and deep pergolas modulate glare without losing connection to the landscape.

On site

Delivery included full interior coordination — lighting scenes, joinery mock-ups, and stone layout drawings reviewed on site before cutting. Archevo Design project directors held weekly site meetings through handover.
