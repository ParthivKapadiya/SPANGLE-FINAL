Sustainable architecture in 2026

Why envelopes, orientation, and material honesty matter more than bolt-on gadgets.

Published January 2026 · Archevo Design

Sustainability in Indian architecture is no longer a niche conversation — it is embedded in how clients evaluate risk, comfort, and long-term operating cost. At Archevo Design we treat performance as part of beauty: a façade that manages sun gracefully is simply better design.

Envelope first

Before photovoltaics or smart glass, we optimise wall-to-window ratio, shading depth, and thermal mass for the specific microclimate. In Gujarat, that often means generous overhangs, courtyards for stack ventilation, and high-performance glazing only where the view demands it.

Materials that age in place

We favour stone, timber from certified sources, lime-based plasters, and finishes that can be maintained locally. Replacing a failed imported coating in five years is not sustainable — choosing patina-friendly surfaces is.

Water and landscape

Rainwater harvesting, permeable edges, and native planting reduce mechanical load on the building while improving everyday life. These systems are integrated at schematic stage, not added as a checklist at the end.
