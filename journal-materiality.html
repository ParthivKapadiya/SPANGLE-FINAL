Materiality in Indian light

How limestone, teak, and lime plaster respond to our climate — and how we specify for longevity.

Published October 2025 · Archevo Design

Indian daylight is high-contrast. Materials that look soft in a European catalogue can read flat or chalky here. We test boards on site orientation whenever possible — north light is not south light.

Stone

Kota, Jaisalmer, and imported marbles each carry maintenance expectations. We document sealing cycles and show clients realistic wet-season behaviour before sign-off.

Timber

Teak and oak perform differently under humidity swings. Outdoor joinery gets deeper eaves and ventilation gaps; indoor stair treads are detailed for re-sanding cycles.
